# FlowFrame Trademark Policy

This policy explains how the FlowFrame name, logo, and related marks may be used.
It is meant to keep the community welcoming while avoiding confusion about what
is official, endorsed, or operated by the FlowFrame maintainers.

This document is not legal advice. If you have a specific legal question, consult
your own counsel.

## Protected Marks

The FlowFrame marks include:

- The name `FlowFrame`
- The FlowFrame logo and visual identity
- Product or service names that clearly identify official FlowFrame offerings,
  including names such as `FlowFrame Cloud`, `FlowFrame Marketplace`,
  `FlowFrame Premium`, and similar official-sounding names

## Allowed Uses

You may use the FlowFrame name without asking for permission when the use is
truthful, descriptive, and not confusing. Examples:

- Referring to the FlowFrame open-source project.
- Saying that your plugin, tutorial, template, integration, or service works
  with FlowFrame.
- Naming a package in a descriptive way such as `plugin for FlowFrame` or
  `Acme connector for FlowFrame`.
- Linking to the FlowFrame repository or documentation.
- Writing reviews, tutorials, comparisons, talks, articles, or videos about
  FlowFrame.

## Uses That Need Permission

Please ask before using the FlowFrame marks in ways that could imply official
status, ownership, sponsorship, or endorsement. Examples:

- Naming a hosted service `FlowFrame Cloud`, `FlowFrame Hosted`, or a similar
  official-sounding name.
- Using the FlowFrame logo as the main branding for your company, product,
  plugin marketplace, or paid service.
- Registering domains, social accounts, packages, organizations, or apps that
  make it look like you are the official FlowFrame project.
- Using `FlowFrame` as the first or dominant word in a commercial product name.
- Modifying the FlowFrame logo in a way that creates a confusingly similar mark.

## Plugins and Integrations

Plugin authors may describe compatibility with FlowFrame and may choose their own
plugin licenses. However:

- Do not call a plugin "official" unless it is published or explicitly endorsed
  by the FlowFrame maintainers.
- Do not imply that a community or third-party plugin is maintained, reviewed, or
  supported by the FlowFrame project unless that is true.
- Prefer names such as `Acme Snowflake Connector for FlowFrame` over
  `FlowFrame Snowflake Connector` unless the plugin is official.

## Forks and Modified Versions

You may fork and modify the FlowFrame code under the applicable open-source
licenses. If you distribute a modified version, make it clear that it is modified
and not the official FlowFrame release.

Recommended wording:

> This is a modified distribution of FlowFrame and is not affiliated with or
> endorsed by the official FlowFrame maintainers.

## Reporting Misuse

If you see confusing or misleading use of the FlowFrame name or logo, please open
a discussion or contact the maintainers with enough context to review it.

The maintainers may update this policy as the project grows.
